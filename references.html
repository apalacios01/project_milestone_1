References and Resources

The following sources were used to better understand how cookies function in web development and how they impact user privacy. I selected a mix of technical, legal, and real-world sources to get a more complete understanding of the topic.

Website Sources

  • GeeksforGeeks - What Are Internet Cookies?

    This source helped explain how cookies work from a technical perspective, including how they store user data and support website functionality.

  • GDPR.eu - Cookies and GDPR

    This source provided information on privacy laws and how cookies are regulated, especially in terms of user consent and data protection.

  • Kaspersky - What Are Cookies?
    This resource explained cookies from a security perspective, including how they identify users and store information in the browser.
  • FTC - Internet Cookies
    This source added insight into how cookies are used in the United States and how websites track user activity and behavior.
  • Cloudflare - What Are Cookies?

    This source gave a clear and simple explanation of cookies, including how they help websites maintain sessions and improve user experience.

  • MDN Web Docs - Using HTTP Cookies

    This source helped explain the technical side of HTTP cookies, including how cookies are stored and used by websites.

  • MDN Web Docs - Set-Cookie Header

    This source helped explain how servers send cookies to browsers using the Set-Cookie header.

  • European Commission - Cookies Policy

    This source shows how cookies are explained in real privacy policies and how they are used in practice.

  • Cookie Law - Overview

    This source helped explain how cookie laws work globally and how websites must inform users about data collection.

  • W3Schools - JavaScript Cookies

    This resource showed how cookies are created and used in JavaScript, which helped connect the concept to web development.

Video Sources

  • What are cookies? Website Cookies explained in 2 minutes!
    This video helped simplify the concept of cookies and showed how they are used in a real-world browsing experience.
  • How Cookies Can Track You
    This video focused more on privacy concerns, explaining how cookies can track users across multiple websites.
