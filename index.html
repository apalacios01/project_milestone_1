Introduction

Cookies are a major part of how websites create personalized experiences for users. They help websites remember logins, save preferences, and improve convenience. At the same time, cookies have also become part of larger conversations about online privacy, data collection, and how much information users are giving away without fully realizing it.

I chose this topic because cookies seem simple at first, but they have a much bigger effect on the web than most people think. They influence convenience, advertising, analytics, and privacy, which makes them an important part of modern web development.

Why This Topic Matters

In web development, understanding cookies is important because developers often use them to improve user experience. However, developers also have a responsibility to think about privacy, transparency, and ethical data use. A website should not only function well, but also respect the user.
